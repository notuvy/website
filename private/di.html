1993 Daily Illini Articles

In the Spring Semester of 1993 (my final term as a graduate student), I worked as a reporter for the campus newpaper, "The Daily Illini." I wrote a weekly column in which I interviewed a university professor to discuss their research. The purpose was to provide a picture of the various research projects taking place around the campus. I chose professors from a variety of disciplines in order to expose a diverse range of projects.

  • Jan 29 - Professor studies language understanding.
  • Feb 5 - Insects help robotic industry.
  • Feb 12 - Advertising prof studies motivation for recycling habits.
  • Feb 19 - Professor studies improvement of public-housing environments.
  • Feb 26 - Professor studying past to understand human ecology.
  • Mar 5 - Students try to give spark to auto industry.
  • Mar 19 - UI biologist researches plant growth.
  • Mar 26 - Prof researches drug testing in horses.
  • Apr 2 - Researcher says water shortages can drown international relations.
  • Apr 15 - Study shows GPAs might drop under new policy.
  • Apr 29 - UI prof paints portrait of art and education.
  • May 7 - Researcher analyzes finances in elections.
